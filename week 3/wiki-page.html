The Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northen Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently "Least Concern." There are manhy subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Here are some bear species:

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (extinct)

The following countries have the largest populations of brown bears:

  1. Russia
  2. United States
  3. Canada
